The program operates on a credit hour system, with the academic year split into two semesters, and the possibility of an additional summer term.

To graduate, a student must successfully complete 129 credit hours across at least eight semesters, excluding summer terms. These semesters are distributed over four academic levels.

Students can register for a minimum of 9 credit hours and a maximum of 18 credit hours per semester. Exceptions may be made for graduation cases with the approval of the program supervisor.

The student must have a General Certificate of Secondary Education (GCSE) in the same academic year of admission.

To be admitted, you must first be accepted into the Faculty of Commerce and Business Administration as a regular (full-time) student through the Coordination Office in the same year of admission. Please note that external students are not accepted into the Business Information Systems program.

The minimum score for English proficiency will be determined based on coordination and admission indicators. Additionally, preference is given to applicants with English as their first foreign language.

Student selection is based on the total sum of grades obtained, including secondary education total grade, English language subject grade, and high-level degree in English language subject if available.
